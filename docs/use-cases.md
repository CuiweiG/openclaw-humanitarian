## Real-World Use Cases

### 1. Crisis Information Translation
UN agencies publish 50+ situation reports daily — almost exclusively in English. Local responders and affected civilians in Iran, Lebanon, and Afghanistan can't read them. Our pipeline auto-translates key bulletins into 9 languages within hours of publication.

*Impact: A WFP food distribution schedule translated into Dari can reach 33,000 Afghan refugees who would otherwise miss it.*

### 2. Diaspora Family Connection
Millions of Iranians abroad have lost contact with family during the 28-day internet blackout. Our bot provides verified guidance for reaching family via landline, satellite phone hotlines (ICRC), and third-country intermediaries.

*Impact: A practical guide in Dari helps Afghan refugees in Iran contact family in Kabul when WhatsApp is blocked.*

### 3. Shelter Capacity Tracking *(In Development)*
645 collective shelters in Lebanon, most at or over capacity. Displaced families travel between full shelters wasting precious time and energy. We're building a crowdsourced real-time tracker — shelter workers report via Telegram, civilians query nearest available space.

*Technical approach: Telegram bot commands + Haversine distance calculation + crowdsourced data.*

### 4. Offline Information Distribution *(Research Phase)*
When governments cut internet, civilians lose access to life-saving information. We're designing a Briar (Bluetooth/WiFi mesh) + Meshtastic (LoRa radio) layered architecture that delivers humanitarian bulletins without any internet connection.

*Why this matters: Iran's internet has been cut for 28+ days. 90 million people have no online access to evacuation routes or shelter locations.*

### 5. Multilingual Early Warning Relay
Evacuation warnings are often issued via social media platforms that are blocked or inaccessible in conflict zones. Our system can relay verified warnings through offline channels — mesh radio, SMS where available, and shortwave broadcast partners.

### 6. Humanitarian Worker Language Support
Local aid workers frequently receive English-only coordination documents from UN OCHA, WFP, and UNHCR. Our translation pipeline integrates with ReliefWeb's public API to auto-translate new publications as they appear.

### 7. Post-Conflict Recovery Information
When conflicts end, millions need information about: returning home safely (unexploded ordnance risks), accessing reconstruction aid, and finding missing family members (ICRC Restoring Family Links). Our system will pre-translate these guides into all 9 supported languages.
